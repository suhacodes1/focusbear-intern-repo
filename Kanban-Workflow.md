# Agile Workflows & Kanban

## Research & Learn

### 1. How a Kanban Board Works

A Kanban board is a visual tool used to manage tasks and workflows. Tasks are represented as cards and move across columns that represent different stages of work. This helps teams track progress and manage workload efficiently.

---

### 2. Meaning of Kanban Columns

- **Backlog / Not Started:** Tasks that are yet to begin
- **In Progress:** Tasks currently being worked on
- **Blocked:** Tasks that cannot proceed due to issues
- **Ready for Review:** Tasks completed and awaiting feedback
- **Approved / Done:** Tasks that are completed and finalised

---

### 3. Task Movement and Responsibility

Tasks move from left to right across the board as progress is made.
The person working on the task is responsible for updating its status to reflect its current stage.

---

### 4. Benefits of Limiting Work in Progress (WIP)

- Prevents taking on too many tasks at once
- Improves focus and productivity
- Reduces task-switching and inefficiency
- Helps identify bottlenecks in workflow

---

## Reflection

### 1. How Kanban Helps Manage Priorities

Kanban helps prioritise tasks by clearly showing what needs to be done, what is in progress, and what is completed. It reduces overload by encouraging focus on fewer tasks at a time.

---

### 2. Improving Workflow Using Kanban

I can improve my workflow by:

- Focusing on completing tasks before starting new ones
- Keeping task statuses updated regularly
- Breaking large tasks into smaller, manageable steps
