# Working with Vulnerable Populations

Research & Learn

1. Vulnerable Populations and Challenges in Digital Spaces

Vulnerable populations include individuals who may face increased barriers in accessing or using digital tools. This includes people with ADHD, Autism, cognitive impairments, mental health challenges, or limited digital literacy.

Common challenges include:

Difficulty focusing or maintaining attention
Cognitive overload from complex interfaces
Sensory sensitivity (e.g., bright colours, animations, noise)
Confusion due to unclear navigation or instructions
Frustration when tasks require sustained effort or multiple steps 2. Ethical Considerations for Neurodivergent Individuals
Design with empathy and avoid assumptions about user behaviour
Minimise cognitive load by simplifying interfaces and instructions
Avoid overwhelming users with excessive information or visual stimuli
Ensure transparency and clarity in communication
Respect user autonomy and avoid manipulative or coercive design patterns
Prioritise accessibility and inclusivity in all design decisions 3. Making Content and Interactions More Accessible
Use simple, clear, and direct language
Break tasks into smaller, manageable steps
Maintain consistent and predictable navigation
Reduce distractions such as unnecessary animations or clutter
Provide clear feedback and progress indicators
Allow flexibility in how users complete tasks 4. Supporting Neurodivergent Team Members
Communicate clearly and avoid ambiguity
Provide structured instructions and expectations
Respect different working styles and pacing
Be patient and open to different communication preferences
Allow flexibility in deadlines and workflows where possible
Create a supportive and non judgemental environment

1. Adjusting Communication Style

To be more inclusive, I will:

Use clear and concise language
Avoid vague instructions or assumptions
Provide step-by-step explanations when needed
Be patient and supportive in tone
Check for understanding rather than assuming clarity 2. Common UX and Communication Pitfalls
Overly complex interfaces with too many options
Lack of clear instructions or guidance
Inconsistent navigation or layout
Excessive notifications or distractions
Using technical or ambiguous language
Not considering different attention spans or processing speeds 3. Practical Change to Support Vulnerable Populations

One practical change I can make is to break down tasks and instructions into smaller, clearly defined steps to reduce cognitive load and improve usability.

Task Completion

1. Key Insight from First-Person Account

A common insight from individuals with ADHD is that productivity tools can feel overwhelming when they require too many steps or present too much information at once. Simplicity, clarity, and flexibility are essential for maintaining engagement.

1. Design or Communication Improvement

I will focus on reducing cognitive overload by ensuring that interfaces and instructions are simple, structured, and easy to follow. This includes limiting unnecessary elements and prioritising clarity.

1. Example Supportive Response to a User

"Thank you for sharing your experience. It is completely understandable to feel overwhelmed when tasks start to build up. Let’s try breaking things down into smaller, manageable steps. You can start with just one simple task, and we can take it from there. If anything feels unclear or difficult, please let me know and I can help guide you through it."

Summary

Designing for vulnerable populations requires empathy, clarity, and intentional simplicity. By improving communication, reducing cognitive load, and creating flexible and supportive systems, I can contribute to a more inclusive and effective user experience for both users and team members.
