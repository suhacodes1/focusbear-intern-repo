# Occupational Health & Safety (OHS) – Laptop Ergonomics

## Research & Learn

### 1. What are the risks of using a laptop without an external monitor or keyboard?

Using a laptop without proper ergonomic setup can lead to:

- Poor posture (hunching over the screen)
- Neck and shoulder strain due to looking down
- Wrist strain from awkward typing angles
- Eye strain from improper screen distance and glare
- Increased risk of long-term musculoskeletal issues

---

### 2. What ergonomic equipment can improve posture when working on a laptop?

The following equipment can significantly improve posture and comfort:

- **Laptop stand** → raises screen to eye level
- **External keyboard** → allows proper arm and wrist positioning
- **External mouse** → reduces wrist strain
- **Ergonomic chair** → supports lower back (lumbar support)
- **External monitor** → provides better screen positioning
- **Footrest (optional)** → supports proper leg positioning

---

### 3. What adjustments should be made for a healthier workspace?

**Monitor / Screen:**

- Top of the screen should be at or slightly below eye level
- Screen should be about an arm’s length away

**Chair:**

- Feet flat on the ground
- Knees at a 90-degree angle
- Lower back supported

**Desk / Arms:**

- Elbows at ~90 degrees
- Wrists straight (not bent up/down)
- Shoulders relaxed (not raised)

---

### 4. What daily habits reduce strain from prolonged laptop use?

- Take breaks every 30–60 minutes
- Follow the **20-20-20 rule** (every 20 minutes, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds)
- Stretch neck, shoulders, and wrists regularly
- Sit upright and avoid slouching
- Blink often to prevent dry eyes
- Alternate between sitting and standing (if possible)
