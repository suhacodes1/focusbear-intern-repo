# Data Privacy & Confidentiality

## Research & Learn

### 1. Key Takeaways from Focus Bear’s Privacy Policy

- User data must be collected and used responsibly and only for intended purposes
- Personal and sensitive data must be protected from unauthorised access
- Data should not be shared without proper consent or legal basis
- Security measures must be implemented to prevent breaches
- Users have the right to privacy and transparency regarding how their data is used

---

### 2. Types of Confidential Data at Focus Bear

- User personal information (e.g., names, emails, usage data)
- Company internal documents and strategies
- Product-related data and proprietary systems
- Authentication credentials and access tokens
- Any non-public business or operational information

---

### 3. Best Practices for Handling Confidential Data

- Use strong, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication
- Avoid storing sensitive data in unsecured locations
- Share data only with authorised individuals on a need-to-know basis
- Use secure communication channels when transmitting information
- Lock devices when not in use
- Regularly update software and systems for security patches

---

### 4. Responding to a Data Breach or Accidental Disclosure

- Immediately report the incident to a supervisor or appropriate authority
- Provide clear details about what happened and what data was involved
- Avoid attempting to conceal or fix the issue independently without guidance
- Follow company procedures for incident response
- Take steps to prevent further exposure if possible

---

## Reflection

### 1. Steps to Ensure Secure Data Handling in Daily Tasks

- Be mindful of where and how data is accessed and stored
- Avoid downloading or sharing sensitive information unnecessarily
- Verify recipients before sending any confidential information
- Follow company guidelines for handling and accessing data

---

### 2. Safe Storage, Sharing, and Disposal of Sensitive Information

**Storage:**

- Use secure, company-approved platforms
- Avoid storing sensitive data on personal devices

**Sharing:**

- Share only with authorised individuals
- Use secure and approved communication tools

**Disposal:**

- Permanently delete files when no longer needed
- Avoid leaving sensitive data in accessible or unsecured locations

---

### 3. Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

**Mistakes:**

- Sending data to the wrong recipient
- Using weak or reused passwords
- Storing sensitive data locally without protection
- Clicking on suspicious links or phishing emails

**Prevention:**

- Double-check recipients before sending information
- Use password managers and strong credentials
- Follow secure storage policies
- Stay alert to potential security threats

---
